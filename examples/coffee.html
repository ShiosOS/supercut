How we picked these ads

Advertisers kill losing ads fast, so an ad still delivering after a month has earned a look. We searched 240 ads in this market — including its heaviest spenders, so the pool reflects where real budgets go, not just what got views — and kept the ones running 30+ days and seen in the last 30. An AI watched each surviving ad and answered a fixed set of questions about it; code then counted the answers. 20 ads that turned out to sell something else were rejected — they are listed at the bottom. No single ad is called a winner here. The findings are patterns that repeat across 48 ads from 42 different brands.

What to test next

Problem-Hook Demo

Hypothesis
If we open a product demo with a problem callout instead of a visual hook, we will capture more viewers.
Why this test
Only 2 out of 21 product demos currently use a problem callout hook.
Call it on
Retention rate at 3 seconds
Shot notes
  • Identify a specific coffee problem.
  • Keep pacing fast in the first 10 seconds.
  • Transition immediately into a full demo.
Risk
Losing the visual appeal that drives the 23 ads using visual hooks.
Draft script
Tired of bitter morning coffee? This brewer changes everything. Watch how easy it is to get smooth flavor every time. Shop now.

Humorous Solution

Hypothesis
If we use humor in a problem-solution ad, we will improve engagement.
Why this test
Only 1 out of 8 ads in the ugc problem-solution category uses a humorous tone.
Call it on
Share rate
Shot notes
  • Use a creator-led feel.
  • Exaggerate a common coffee frustration.
  • Ensure it works without sound.
Risk
The humor might distract from the hard call to action used in 7 of 8 ads.
Draft script
My coffee routine was a disaster. Then I found this. Now I'm actually human before noon. Click to fix your mornings.

Sample brand brief

This section shows the brief generator on a made-up brand: “Bloom, a small-batch mushroom coffee brand for busy parents”. In the app, you type your own brand and the tests below are rewritten for it.

Bloom is a small-batch mushroom coffee brand designed to support the energy and focus needs of busy parents.

Bloom can capitalize on the market's preference for product demos, which make up 19 of the 42 brand ads. By combining creator-led energy with fast pacing, Bloom will stand out against the calm emotional tone seen in standard mushroom coffee ads.

Problem-Hook Demo

The idea
A fast-paced demo showing a parent struggling with a typical caffeine crash before switching to Bloom. The ad immediately demonstrates the brewing process to show how Bloom fits a hectic morning routine.
The pattern it leans on
Only 2 out of 21 product demos currently use a problem callout hook.
Opening line
“Tired of the 2 P.M. crash while the kids are still at full speed?”
Draft script
[0-3s] Parent slumped over a table, kids playing in the background. [3-10s] Quick cuts of Bloom powder being stirred into a mug. [10-20s] Parent taking a sip and looking alert. [20-28s] Text: Clean energy for busy parents. Shop now.

Humorous Solution

The idea
A relatable creator-style video where a parent exaggerates their 'pre-Bloom' morning grumpiness versus their post-mushroom coffee productivity. This uses a lighthearted tone to move away from the neutral vibes of most competitive ads.
The pattern it leans on
Only 1 out of 8 ads in the ugc problem-solution category uses a humorous tone.
Opening line
“I was officially banned from talking before my coffee... then I found this.”
Draft script
[0-5s] Creator in pajamas looking like a zombie. [5-15s] They drink Bloom and suddenly start folding a mountain of laundry at high speed. [15-30s] Calm energy without the jitters. Fix your mornings now at the link below.

Social Proof Lifestyle

The idea
An aspirational montage of a parent’s 'peaceful' moments—early morning reading or a quick workout—overlayed with real customer testimonials. This builds trust by showing the product actually works for the demographic.
The pattern it leans on
Only 2 out of 8 lifestyle montages currently use social proof as a hook.
Opening line
“Join 50,000 parents who finally found their morning focus.”
Draft script
[0-5s] 5-star rating overlayed on a shot of steam rising from a cup. [5-15s] Quick clips of a parent looking focused and energetic during daily tasks. [15-21s] Bloom bag on screen with a final review quote: 'The only thing that gets me through.' Shop Bloom now.

How reliable was the watching?

We measured the editing pace of every ad twice: once by the AI's eye, once by scene-detection software. The AI was off by 2.4 cuts on average, and within 2 cuts of the software on 60% of ads. The cut counts printed in the chapters are the software's, not the AI's.

Where a spoken hook could be checked against the ad library's own transcript (14 ads), the AI's quote matched 14 times and disagreed 0 times. The other 34 ads had on-screen-text hooks, no hook, or no transcript — nothing to check against.

11 of 48 ads were described by the AI as making no ask, yet the ad unit itself carries a purchase button (ads fe9bd9fc3912b313915b0, f3ceb491247f019424154, fe0ae602e19999a020122, fdf35298e62c9e0accb1b, f49ffb94892baea95ba28, f1ae323c4179af71305e5, f7b7f015392c915b8d6ff, f3363d3be4d9d65afc492, f4ffda667dbf9e12cee57, afc8513033f430404a49, ff5bf764d6056e9db82fc). Read those "no ask" counts with that in mind: the AI judges the video, not the button under it.

Assumptions this report makes

  • "Still delivering" means the ad was seen in the ad library within the last 30 days. The data offers no direct "currently live" flag.
  • Days-running and play counts come from the ad library and are taken at face value.
  • A long-running ad earned study, but nothing here proves any single ad was profitable. Only the repeated patterns are conclusions.
  • Spoken quotes were transcribed by the AI from the audio; where the ad library had its own transcript, we cross-checked (see above).

Study ranking: ads are ordered for the example lists by a score of longevity ×0.5 + engagement ×0.3 + formatConfidence ×0.2, each part normalized within this pool, with at most one ad per brand in any list. Engagement is measured as a rate — likes and shares per play, a share counting 5× a like — because plays on a paid ad are bought, so raw counts would mostly rank budgets. Rates from under 10,000 plays are ignored as noise.

Made with Supercut. Days-running and play counts come from the ad library data; "still delivering" means seen in the last 30 days, the closest signal the data offers to "currently live".
