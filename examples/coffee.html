How we picked these ads

Advertisers kill losing ads fast, so an ad still delivering after a month has earned a look. We searched 234 ads in this market — including its heaviest spenders, so the pool reflects where real budgets go, not just what got views — and kept the ones running 30+ days and seen in the last 30. An AI watched each surviving ad and answered a fixed set of questions about it; code then counted the answers. 13 ads that turned out to sell something else were rejected — they are listed at the bottom. No single ad is called a winner here. The findings are patterns that repeat across 52 ads from 41 different brands.

What to test next

Visual Hook vs. Problem Callout in Demos

Hypothesis
If we use a visual hook instead of a problem callout in product demos, we will see longer watch times.
Why this test
Visual hooks are used in 19 out of 25 product demo ads, while problem callouts are only used in 2.
Call it on
Average view duration
Shot notes
  • Record a close-up of coffee pouring.
  • Record a creator mentioning a bad morning routine.
  • Ensure both show the product in the first 3 seconds.
  • Add energetic music to both edits.
Risk
A visual hook might look like generic content and get ignored.
Draft script
Watch this perfect crema pour. This is how you start every morning. Shop the espresso maker now.

Creator vs. Brand Feel for Brewing Equipment

Hypothesis
If we use a creator feel instead of a brand feel, we will increase click-through rates.
Why this test
Creator feel is the most popular style across the whole pool with 22 out of 52 ads.
Call it on
Click-through rate
Shot notes
  • Film a creator in a home kitchen setting.
  • Film a studio-style brand montage.
  • Use a hard CTA for both versions.
  • Keep pacing at a medium speed.
Risk
Creator footage may look too low-quality for an expensive equipment brand.
Draft script
I finally found a way to make cafe-quality lattes at home. It is so easy. Click shop now to see how.

Hard CTA vs. No CTA in Lifestyle Montages

Hypothesis
If we add a hard CTA to lifestyle montages, we will see higher conversion intent.
Why this test
Lifestyle montages are split, with 3 out of 6 using a hard CTA and 2 out of 6 using none.
Call it on
Conversion rate
Shot notes
  • Gather aspirational morning footage.
  • Create an end card with a Shop Now button.
  • Create an end card with just the logo.
  • Bold claims should appear in both hooks.
Risk
A hard CTA can break the emotional aspirational tone of the montage.
Draft script
Elevate your morning ritual to something better. You deserve the best beans. Shop the collection now.

Bloom is a small-batch mushroom coffee brand designed to support the demanding lifestyles of busy parents.

Bloom can capitalize on the market's heavy reliance on visual hooks and high product visibility. By blending a creator feel with energetice tones, the brand can stand out to parents who need both functional benefits and a relatable morning ritual.

How reliable was the watching?

We measured the editing pace of every ad twice: once by the AI's eye, once by scene-detection software. The AI was off by 2.6 cuts on average, and within 2 cuts of the software on 54% of ads. The cut counts printed in the chapters are the software's, not the AI's.

Where a spoken hook could be checked against the ad library's own transcript (13 ads), the AI's quote matched 13 times and disagreed 0 times. The other 39 ads had on-screen-text hooks, no hook, or no transcript — nothing to check against.

10 of 52 ads were described by the AI as making no ask, yet the ad unit itself carries a purchase button (ads f3ceb491247f019424154, fe0ae602e19999a020122, fdf35298e62c9e0accb1b, f49ffb94892baea95ba28, f1ae323c4179af71305e5, f7b7f015392c915b8d6ff, f3363d3be4d9d65afc492, f4ffda667dbf9e12cee57, f345c28707cfeeedb8bab, f7706e5714855be0024b3). Read those "no ask" counts with that in mind: the AI judges the video, not the button under it.

Assumptions this report makes

  • "Still delivering" means the ad was seen in the ad library within the last 30 days. The data offers no direct "currently live" flag.
  • Days-running and play counts come from the ad library and are taken at face value.
  • A long-running ad earned study, but nothing here proves any single ad was profitable. Only the repeated patterns are conclusions.
  • Spoken quotes were transcribed by the AI from the audio; where the ad library had its own transcript, we cross-checked (see above).

Study ranking: ads are ordered for the example lists by a score of longevity ×0.5 + engagement ×0.3 + formatConfidence ×0.2, each part normalized within this pool, with at most one ad per brand in any list. Engagement is measured as a rate — likes and shares per play, a share counting 5× a like — because plays on a paid ad are bought, so raw counts would mostly rank budgets. Rates from under 10,000 plays are ignored as noise.

Made with Supercut. Days-running and play counts come from the ad library data; "still delivering" means seen in the last 30 days, the closest signal the data offers to "currently live".
