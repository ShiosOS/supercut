How we picked these ads

Advertisers kill losing ads fast, so an ad still delivering after a month has earned a look. We searched 234 ads in this market — including its heaviest spenders, so the pool reflects where real budgets go, not just what got views — and kept the ones running 30+ days and seen in the last 30. An AI watched each surviving ad and answered a fixed set of questions about it; code then counted the answers. 8 ads that turned out to sell something else were rejected — they are listed at the bottom. No single ad is called a winner here. The findings are patterns that repeat across 58 ads from 45 different brands.

How to shoot it

  • Show a full demonstration of the product in use. (27 of 28 ads)

What to test next

Silent Social Proof Hook

Hypothesis
If we open a testimonial with a social proof hook instead of a visual hook, we will increase conversion.
Why this test
Social proof hooks are only used in 1 out of 9 creator testimonials despite being a common marketing tactic.
Call it on
Conversion Rate
Shot notes
  • Include a 5-star graphic in the first frame
  • Overlay a quote from a happy customer
  • Keep the creator visible but silent for the first 2 seconds
Risk
The visual may not be as stimulating as a product-heavy opening.
Draft script
Hook: 'The coffee setup everyone is talking about' over 5-star icons. Beat: Creator tastes coffee and nods. Beat: Shot of the machine brewing. CTA: Shop now.

Fast-Paced Lifestyle Aesthetic

Hypothesis
If we increase the edit speed of a lifestyle montage, we will see higher view-through rates.
Why this test
Only 1 out of 7 lifestyle montages currently use fast pacing.
Call it on
Average Watch Time
Shot notes
  • Cut clips every 0.5 to 1 second
  • Match cuts to an energetic beat
  • Show only 3-second glimpses of the product
Risk
The aspirational feeling might be lost if the cuts are too aggressive.
Draft script
Visual: Fast cuts of steam, pouring, drinking, and a sunny kitchen. Text: 'Upgrade your morning.' Beat: Quick logo flash. CTA: Learn more.

Product Demo Curiosity Hook

Hypothesis
If we use a curiosity tease in a product demo, we can stop the scroll more effectively than a standard visual hook.
Why this test
Curiosity teases are rare, used in only 1 out of 28 product demo ads.
Call it on
Hook Rate
Shot notes
  • Hide part of the brewing process with a text overlay
  • Focus on an unusual angle of the machine
  • Ask a question in the text overlay

People may find it confusing if they cannot tell immediately what the product is.

Sample brand brief

This section shows the brief generator on a made-up brand: “Bloom, a small-batch mushroom coffee brand for busy parents”. In the app, you type your own brand and the tests below are rewritten for it.

Bloom is a small-batch mushroom coffee brand designed to support the energy and focus needs of busy parents.

Bloom can capitalize on the market's heavy reliance on product demos while standing out through higher-tempo editing. By blending creator-led energetic tones with fast-paced visual storytelling, Bloom can distinguish its premium mushroom blend from standard brewing equipment competitors.

Silent Social Proof Hook

The idea
A creator-led video opening with a heavy emphasis on parental approval. It features a sleeping baby in the background while the parent enjoys a quiet cup of Bloom coffee, using text overlays for social proof.
The pattern it leans on
Social proof hooks are utilized in only 1 out of 9 creator testimonials despite the segment's 209-day median durability.
Opening line
“The only reason I survived this morning's 5 AM wakeup call.”
Draft script
Visual: 5-star icons appear over a parent taking a blissful first sip of Bloom. Text: 'Thousands of parents agree.' Beat: Extreme close-up of mushroom coffee powder dissolving instantly. Beat: Parent looking focused while working. CTA: Shop Now.

Fast-Paced Lifestyle Aesthetic

The idea
A high-energy montage showing the chaotic transition from morning chaos to productive parent. It uses rapid cuts to show the speed and ritual of brewing a cup of Bloom.
The pattern it leans on
Only 1 out of 7 lifestyle montages currently use fast pacing, representing a significant gap in an aspirational market.
Opening line
“From brain fog to boss mode in 60 seconds.”
Draft script
Visual: 0.5-second cuts of a morning alarm, boiling water, Bloom bag opening, and steam. Beat: Quick shots of a parent playing with kids then typing on a laptop. Beat: Close-up of Bloom bag. CTA: Learn More.

Product Demo Curiosity Hook

The idea
A demo that focuses on the unique, 'small-batch' nature of the mushroom blend. It uses an unusual macro angle to tease the product ingredients before revealing the Bloom brand.
The pattern it leans on

How reliable was the watching?

We measured the editing pace of every ad twice: once by the AI's eye, once by scene-detection software. The AI was off by 2.5 cuts on average, and within 2 cuts of the software on 57% of ads. The cut counts printed in the chapters are the software's, not the AI's.

Where a spoken hook could be checked against the ad library's own transcript (15 ads), the AI's quote matched 15 times and disagreed 0 times. The other 43 ads had on-screen-text hooks, no hook, or no transcript — nothing to check against.

11 of 58 ads were described by the AI as making no ask, yet the ad unit itself carries a purchase button (ads f3ceb491247f019424154, fe0ae602e19999a020122, fdf35298e62c9e0accb1b, f49ffb94892baea95ba28, f1ae323c4179af71305e5, f7b7f015392c915b8d6ff, f3363d3be4d9d65afc492, f4ffda667dbf9e12cee57, f345c28707cfeeedb8bab, f7706e5714855be0024b3, fe9bd9fc3912b313915b0). Read those "no ask" counts with that in mind: the AI judges the video, not the button under it.

Assumptions this report makes

  • "Still delivering" means the ad was seen in the ad library within the last 30 days. The data offers no direct "currently live" flag.
  • Days-running and play counts come from the ad library and are taken at face value.
  • A long-running ad earned study, but nothing here proves any single ad was profitable. Only the repeated patterns are conclusions.
  • Spoken quotes were transcribed by the AI from the audio; where the ad library had its own transcript, we cross-checked (see above).

Study ranking: ads are ordered for the example lists by a score of longevity ×0.5 + engagement ×0.3 + formatConfidence ×0.2, each part normalized within this pool, with at most one ad per brand in any list. Engagement is measured as a rate — likes and shares per play, a share counting 5× a like — because plays on a paid ad are bought, so raw counts would mostly rank budgets. Rates from under 10,000 plays are ignored as noise.
