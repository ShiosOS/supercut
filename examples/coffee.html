How we picked these ads

Advertisers kill losing ads fast, so an ad still delivering after a month has earned a look. We searched 234 ads in this market — including its heaviest spenders, so the pool reflects where real budgets go, not just what got views — and kept the ones running 30+ days and seen in the last 30. An AI watched each surviving ad and answered a fixed set of questions about it; code then counted the answers. 7 ads that turned out to sell something else were rejected — they are listed at the bottom. No single ad is called a winner here. The findings are patterns that repeat across 59 ads from 48 different brands.

What to test next

The Fast Visual Demo

Hypothesis
If we use a fast-paced product demo, we will increase engagement compared to medium-paced ones.
Why this test
Only 2 ads out of 19 in product demo currently use fast pacing.
Call it on
Click-through rate
Shot notes
  • Shoot 5 cuts in the first 10 seconds.
  • Start with a close-up visual hook.
  • Show the full brewing process.
  • End with a soft call to action.
Risk
The viewer might feel the cuts are too jumpy to follow.
Draft script
[Fast cuts of coffee pouring] This is the smoothest cup you will ever make. [Quick shots of beans, water, and steam] Better beans. Better mornings. Tap to shop.

The Hard Offer Image

Hypothesis
If we use an offer-based hook on a still image, we will drive more immediate sales.
Why this test
Only 1 ad out of 4 in still images uses an offer or discount hook.
Call it on
Conversion rate
Shot notes
  • Hero shot of the coffee equipment.
  • Large text overlay with 20% off code.
  • Bold brand colors only.
  • No creator faces.
Risk
Static images may be ignored in high-motion feeds.
Draft script
[Static Image] THE COFFEE UPGRADE YOU NEED. 20% OFF TODAY ONLY. CODE: BREW20. SHOP NOW.

The Question Lead UGC

Hypothesis
If we open a UGC video with a question, we will stop the scroll better than a bold claim.
Why this test
Only 1 ad out of 15 in the UGC category uses a question as a hook.
Call it on
3-second video view rate
Shot notes
  • Creator looks directly into camera.
  • Hold the product while speaking.
  • Energetic tone with medium pacing.
  • Subtitles for the opening question.
Risk
Questions can feel like bait if not answered quickly.
Draft script
Why are you still drinking burnt coffee? [Creator makes a fresh cup] I switched to these beans and never looked back. See why.

Bloom can capitalize on the 206-day median lifespan for product demos while utilizing mushroom coffee's 5-ad market segment. By blending creator-led urgency with high-frequency visual cuts, the brand can stand out among the 59 total ads in the category.

How reliable was the watching?

We measured the editing pace of every ad twice: once by the AI's eye, once by scene-detection software. The AI was off by 2.5 cuts on average, and within 2 cuts of the software on 61% of ads. The cut counts printed in the chapters are the software's, not the AI's.

Where a spoken hook could be checked against the ad library's own transcript (17 ads), the AI's quote matched 17 times and disagreed 0 times. The other 42 ads had on-screen-text hooks, no hook, or no transcript — nothing to check against.

The AI reported firm format labels for every ad in the pool.

Assumptions this report makes

  • "Still delivering" means the ad was seen in the ad library within the last 30 days. The data offers no direct "currently live" flag.
  • Days-running and play counts come from the ad library and are taken at face value.
  • A long-running ad earned study, but nothing here proves any single ad was profitable. Only the repeated patterns are conclusions.
  • Spoken quotes were transcribed by the AI from the audio; where the ad library had its own transcript, we cross-checked (see above).

Study ranking: ads are ordered for the example lists by a score of longevity ×0.5 + engagement ×0.3 + formatConfidence ×0.2, each part normalized within this pool, with at most one ad per brand in any list. Inside the engagement part, one share counts as 100 plays — passing an ad on is a deliberate endorsement, being watched often is not.
