How we picked these ads

Advertisers kill losing ads fast, so an ad still delivering after a month has earned a look. We searched 216 ads in this market — including its heaviest spenders, so the pool reflects where real budgets go, not just what got views — and kept the ones running 30+ days and seen in the last 30. An AI watched each surviving ad and answered a fixed set of questions about it; code then counted the answers. 0 ads that turned out to sell something else were rejected — they are listed at the bottom. No single ad is called a winner here. The findings are patterns that repeat across 60 ads from 45 different brands.

What to test next

Creator-Led Visual Hooks

Hypothesis
If we use a creator to perform a visual hook instead of just showing the product, we will increase engagement.
Why this test
Creator feel is dominant in 47 ads, but visual hooks are only creators for 4 ads in the problem-solution group.
Call it on
Hook Rate
Shot notes
  • Creator stands in a bright kitchen.
  • Creator aggressively shakes a translucent shaker bottle.
  • Product tub is clearly visible in the foreground.
Risk
The shaking motion may look too messy or unprofessional.
Draft script
Stop drinking clumpy protein! Look at how smooth this isolate mixes. Shop now for the best texture.

Bold Claim Messaging for Isolate

Hypothesis
If we use a bold claim hook for Clear Whey Isolate, we will see higher conversion than using a visual hook.
Why this test
Bold claims are used by 17 ads in the pool, but specifically for isolate, 2 ads use visual hooks and 2 use bold claims.
Call it on
Conversion Rate
Shot notes
  • Text overlay states 'The only protein that tastes like juice'.
  • Clear bottle shows liquid that looks like water.
  • Quick cuts between the tub and the poured drink.
Risk
The audience may find the juice taste claim unbelievable.
Draft script
This is 20 grams of protein that actually tastes like fruit juice. No milky aftertaste. Grab yours today.

Fast-Paced Testimonials

Hypothesis
If we increase the cut rate of a creator testimonial to match a product demo, we will increase retention.
Why this test
Product demos use 10 cuts in the first 10 seconds, while testimonials average only 2.5 cuts.
Call it on
Retention at 10 Seconds
Shot notes
  • Creator speaks while cutting between different angles of their kitchen.
  • Overlay close-ups of the powder scoop every 2 seconds.
  • Switch from creator face to creator drinking at the 5 second mark.
Risk
High cut rates can feel disorienting during a personal testimonial.
Draft script
I've tried every powder out there and this is the one I keep coming back to. It tastes amazing and has clean ingredients. Check it out.

What to double-check

Everything above comes from an AI watching ads one at a time and code counting its answers. Here is where that process was noisy, and what it threw out.

Ads rejected as off-market (0)

The search also returned ads selling something else. They were dropped before any counting, so they do not touch the numbers above.

Nothing was rejected in this run.

How reliable was the watching?

We measured the editing pace of every ad twice: once by the AI's eye, once by scene-detection software. The AI was off by 2.4 cuts on average, and within 2 cuts of the software on 65% of ads. The cut counts printed in the chapters are the software's, not the AI's.

Where a spoken hook could be checked against the ad library's own transcript (21 ads), the AI's quote matched 20 times and disagreed 1 time (ad f2acbfe8687de138601ee). The other 39 ads had on-screen-text hooks, no hook, or no transcript — nothing to check against.

11 of 60 ads were described by the AI as making no ask, yet the ad unit itself carries a purchase button (ads f66d9c73952b213626aca, f0c9fbd9dc6b06d624ebc, f4cb12b210bc99cd00cd2, f42800576e2de20271fa0, f7abe59147eeb4aa56ab2, f88dbc6e2bec95f3e5274, f76bb2743039342506bc7, ff0b38aa61bfbb3e935db, fdc8b6f3ddcc4216a9cbb, b13c9ab4748e89cc7abb, afc8513033f430404a49). Read those "no ask" counts with that in mind: the AI judges the video, not the button under it.

Assumptions this report makes

  • "Still delivering" means the ad was seen in the ad library within the last 30 days. The data offers no direct "currently live" flag.
  • Days-running and play counts come from the ad library and are taken at face value.
  • A long-running ad earned study, but nothing here proves any single ad was profitable. Only the repeated patterns are conclusions.
  • Spoken quotes were transcribed by the AI from the audio; where the ad library had its own transcript, we cross-checked (see above).

Study ranking: ads are ordered for the example lists by a score of longevity ×0.5 + engagement ×0.3 + formatConfidence ×0.2, each part normalized within this pool, with at most one ad per brand in any list. Engagement is measured as a rate — likes and shares per play, a share counting 5× a like — because plays on a paid ad are bought, so raw counts would mostly rank budgets. Rates from under 10,000 plays are ignored as noise.
